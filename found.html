🔴 72-HOUR LEGAL DEADLINE
Miami-Dade County law requires you to:
1. Notify Animal Services, AND
2. Have the pet scanned for a microchip

Both must be done within 72 hours of finding the pet. Failure may result in a civil penalty.
⏱️ Your 72 hours started when you found the pet.

⚠️ You are not authorized to give the pet to another person if no owner comes forward. If the owner is not located, the pet must be surrendered to Miami-Dade Animal Services. Any interested party who wishes to adopt the pet must apply through the official adoption process at the shelter.

Phase 3 — Today
3

Post publicly so the owner can find you

Use this exact wording pattern. Unclear posts slow everything down.

✅ Write: "I HAVE FOUND A DOG — [Neighborhood] — [Date]"

❌ Do not write: "Lost dog" — that means your dog is missing, which is the opposite

Include: photo, color, breed or mix, where found, your phone number in the description

Do not include: your home address

⚠️ Important tip — add your phone number directly in the post description. Petco Love Lost and PawBoost hide your contact info and send notifications by email — which frequently land in spam. Put your number in the body of every post.

Phase 4 — Within 72 Hours Required by law
4

File the official report — and keep the dog with you

⚠️ Miami-Dade County law requires you to notify Animal Services within 72 hours. Failure to do so may result in a civil penalty.

The goal is to keep this dog out of the shelter. Miami-Dade's shelter system is at or beyond capacity. A dog in a home is safer, calmer, and has a much better chance of being reunited with its owner than a dog in an intake facility. You are doing the right thing by fostering.

⏱️ If you are nearing 72 hours, have not found the owner, and are unable to continue fostering, go to Miami-Dade Animal Services in person to complete a foster application — this keeps the dog legally in your care beyond the 72-hour window.

Phase 5 — While You Wait
5

Keep the dog safe

You are legally allowed to keep the dog in your home while you search for the owner. You do not have to take the dog to the shelter.
⚠️ Rabies safety: If you are unaware of the pet's rabies vaccination status and it is showing signs of aggressive behavior, do not keep it at home. Take it to Miami-Dade Animal Services or call 311 immediately.
