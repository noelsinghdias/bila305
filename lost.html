Please note: Leaving your dog without a leash or unsupervised on the streets is against the law in Miami-Dade County. But we understand that no matter how vigilant, mistakes can happen and dogs can escape. It is the owner's responsibility to have your dog microchipped and rabies tagged — and preferably air tagged or GPS tracked for maximum protection. We don't mean to shame, but we want to ensure everyone is aware of the guidelines. The following section should help you find your dog.

⚠️ Miami-Dade County law: The shelter can only hold your dog for 3 days before they become available for adoption. Filing a report today protects your dog.

Phase 3 — Today Legally important — do not skip
3

File a report with Miami-Dade Animal Services

⚠️ Filing an official report creates a record. This is your legal protection if your dog ends up at the shelter — and the clock is short. The shelter can only hold your dog for 3 days before they become eligible for adoption.
🏠 If a finder has your dog, ask them to keep fostering it at home while you work to reunite.

File your report (do this even if you think someone has the dog — it creates an official record):

  • 📞 Call: 311 or 305-468-5900
  • ✉️ Email: asdlostandfound@miamidade.gov
  • 📍 In person: 3599 NW 79 Avenue, Doral FL 33122
  • Mon–Fri: 10:00 AM–6:30 PM | Sat–Sun: 10:00 AM–4:00 PM | Closed county holidays
