Community Help

You don't have to do everything. Pick ONE thing.

The Miami-Dade shelter is currently overwhelmed and we need community support with the following problems:

Problem 1 — No structure or workflow: When someone posts "I lost my pet" or "I found a pet," 40 people reply with conflicting advice and the real actions never happen. There is no shared protocol.

Problem 2 — Scattered resources: The tools exist (Petco Love Lost, PawBoost, the county microchip lookup) but most people don't know about them. Information is fragmented across Facebook groups, NextDoor, and outdated blog posts.

This community-based protocol is put together with the intention to provide a step-by-step resource guide for the Miami-Dade community to follow collectively. The highest-leverage action for most community members is not fostering or advocating — it is replacing your own advice with this protocol. That behavior change scales instantly and costs nothing.

📢

SHARE THE PROTOCOL

2 minutes, free — highest impact

When you see someone posting "I lost my pet" or "I found a pet" — stop typing your own advice and share this link instead.

/lost for someone who lost a pet

/found for someone who found one

One clear protocol beats 40 conflicting comments. Every time someone shares this link instead of their own advice, the community gets a little more coordinated.

🏠🐾

FOSTER OR ADOPT

Days, weeks, or forever

Foster: Temporarily keep a found pet safe in your home while an owner is located or a permanent home is arranged. Post your willingness to foster on NextDoor or local Facebook groups — your neighborhood network is often the fastest way to place a pet quickly.

Adopt: Miami-Dade shelters are at or above capacity. Adoption directly creates space for pets brought in as found strays.

See pets available to adopt — Miami-Dade
📣

ADVOCATE

Ongoing — in your community

The most preventable lost pet situations happen because owners don't know the law. Help educate your community:

  • Remind neighbors: microchipping is the single most effective reunification tool — and must be registered to your current phone number
  • Share the leash law: pets cannot be left unattended on public streets in Miami-Dade County
  • Encourage rabies tagging — it gives finders an immediate way to call Animal Services or 311 and trace the owner

A microchipped, licensed, tagged pet can be traced to its owner in minutes. An unregistered pet with no tag can take weeks — or never make it home.
